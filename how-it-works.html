✦ HOW IT WORKS ✦

From posting a brief to completing a trade, TradeAux turns fragmented global trade into a connected process.

01
🧩 Kontors Structure Demand and Supply
Trade begins inside Kontors (based on the Hanseatic League trade node)—structured trade environments where buyers and sellers exist in the same ecosystem. Products, availability, and demand signals are organized by category, origin, and market activity.
Example: "Example: Coconut exporters in a Kontor become visible to buyers operating in the same trade network."
02
🤖 The Market Comes Alive
Once an auction is posted, on that day, the system opens it to active bidding. Buyers and sellers respond in real time using automated trading bots strategized by the buyer. Competition begins immediately as pricing adjusts dynamically based on interest and supply.
Trading doesn’t wait for business hours or time zones. Bots never sleep.
03
⚖️ Offers Compete, Not Just Prices
All bids remain visible so participants understand the full market picture. Sellers evaluate multiple offers and choose which ones to accept. Buyers can win partial or full allocations depending on how supply is distributed.
Instead of a single winner, multiple matches can clear products in one cycle.
04
🚛 Move the Goods
Once a trade is accepted, logistics becomes the next coordinated step. Carriers compete to move cargo from origin to destination through a transport auction. Different transport modes participate to find the most efficient route and price.
Buyer pays $25. Winning carrier gives $50 discount off invoice.
05
💰 SIMPLE TRANSACTION MODEL
Each completed transaction follows a clear structure. A flat fee of $25 is applied when an offer is accepted. The seller provides the buyer with a $50 discount off his invoice. This keeps pricing transparent and consistent across the platform.
No percentage commissions. No hidden layers. Just a simple execution cost.
06
💳 Receivables Auction
After a trade is completed, the Bill of Lading is issued and the cargo is under way, sellers can optionally auction their receivables for early redemption. Financial participants compete to offer the best terms, allowing faster access to liquidity without changing the underlying trade.
Quick settlements means money for your business.
